About Me

I grew up in the small town of Shoemakersville, PA. I went to a smaller public high school that didn't offer any programming classes and I never even thought that I might enjoy it until I tried it. Looking back now it makes a lot of sense, I have always been a logical and inquisitive person with an affinity for problem solving -- my friends and I used to solve rubik's cubes for fun -- . Anyway, I enrolled at the University of Pittsburgh as a Chemical Engineering student in 2014. The engineering curriculum at Pitt teaches all freshman students a little bit of basic programming. Even with this tiny amount of exposure to programming I soon decided that it was what I really wanted to do and I changed my major.

Since changing my major I've developed an incredible passion for programming. I love all of my programming classes and I try to learn as much as I can outside of the classroom too. I've also done some undergraduate research, in the field of Web Content Blocking. Sometimes I feel that I started my programming career with a bit of a handicap. Many of the students in my field/major have known that this is what they wanted to do for quite some time, and have been developing their skills that entire time. But I feel that I've caught up with these students very quickly by studying dilligently and learning outside of the classroom.

When I'm not working/learning I try to stay as active as possible. I spend a lot of time exploring outside, my favorite hobbies are whitewater kayaking, skiing, backpacking, and rock climbing. Whitewater kayaking is by far my favorite of those three and it's a pretty big part of my life. I started whitewater kayaking when I was 16 and I loved it from the start. It is incredibly fun and it puts me in beautiful places where I can enjoy and connect with nature.
